01 / Journey

The pattern in my journey is growth through discomfort.

I have often grown the most when I chose not to stay where things felt easy. Looking back, each phase of my journey shaped me in a different way — through service, leadership, engineering, and challenge.

Secondary School • Teaching & Community Service

Teaching first showed me that growth is most meaningful when it helps someone else grow too.

Working with younger students made me more patient, more adaptable, and more aware of how much thoughtful guidance can change a student’s confidence and mindset.

JC1 – JC2 • My growth

I started stepping out of my comfort zone more deliberately.

Balancing academics with cricket, entrepreneurship, volunteering, and flying stretched me in every direction, but it also made me more disciplined, resilient, and intentional about how I used my time.

2023 • Engineering Innovation Challenge

EIC was when engineering stopped feeling abstract to me.

After seeing my grandfather struggle following a spinal injury, working on elderly loneliness made engineering feel personal. It was the first time I saw how technical ideas could respond to a deeply human problem.

2023-2024 • Singapore Youth Flying Club

Flight training taught me composure, discipline, and responsibility in a way nothing else had before.

SYFC demanded precision, consistency, and calm under pressure. Over time, it changed how I approach uncertainty, setbacks, and high-stakes decisions.

2024 • Raffles Entrepreneurs' Network

Leadership became less about directing people and more about serving them well.

Leading EntreWeek taught me that strong teams are built through trust, communication, and giving people real ownership over their work, not by trying to control everything yourself.

National Service • Global Impact

Developing WebForce during National Service taught me what it means to build for people who need reliability.

Developing something for live performance made me think differently about responsibility, precision, and trust. It showed me that building well is not just about having an idea, but about making something people can genuinely rely on when it matters.

Moving Forward • Continuous Growth

What ties these experiences together is the kind of person I am trying to become.

I want to keep growing in environments that are demanding, meaningful, and bigger than myself — where I can keep learning, contribute seriously, and build things that matter to people.

Across flying, engineering, and building products, I have seen that real problems are rarely isolated. I want to learn how to approach them with both technical depth and a broader, systems-level perspective.

05 / The Person
Garv Batra

The person
behind the work.

I think what drives me most is the need to be close to real problems, not just talk about them from a distance.

Over time, different experiences have shaped how I approach that. Flying made me more aware of responsibility and how quickly situations can change. Building things showed me that ideas are easy, but making something actually work for people takes patience and iteration. Teaching reminded me that progress is often quiet, and that helping someone understand something can matter more than any visible outcome.

I am still figuring things out, but I know I am drawn to environments where the work is real, the expectations are high, and there is something at stake. I find that I grow the most in those situations.

More than anything, I want to become someone people can rely on — not just to have ideas, but to take responsibility and see things through properly.
