Personal Reflection

The ideas that connect my experiences.

How flying shaped my problem-solving

Flying taught me that pressure does not excuse poor thinking. It demands better thinking. In the cockpit, hesitation, overconfidence, and sloppy preparation have consequences. That mindset has carried into how I study, build, and lead.

Why I am drawn to engineering

I am fascinated by how complex systems become usable realities. Engineering, to me, is not abstract theory alone. It is disciplined creativity applied to make difficult things work reliably in the real world.

Why building matters to me

I do not just want to understand ideas. I want to test them, refine them, and turn them into things that people can actually use. That is why product-building, entrepreneurship, and technical problem-solving appeal to me so strongly.

What I value in a learning environment

I am looking for a place that does not separate technical depth from initiative, global perspective, and execution. I grow most in environments that are rigorous, interdisciplinary, and demanding.

Why NTU REP

REP aligns with how I want to grow and what I want to build.

What draws me most to REP is its refusal to keep engineering isolated from leadership, business thinking, and global exposure. I am applying to engineering not only because I enjoy systems and technical problem-solving, but because I want to use them to build solutions that matter beyond the classroom.

Why the programme fits me

  • The engineering rigour matches my appetite for demanding, high-discipline environments.
  • The business dimension reflects my longstanding interest in entrepreneurship and building useful products.
  • The overseas immersion component aligns with my desire to learn from broader systems, cultures, and markets.
  • The interdisciplinary nature of REP mirrors the way I already think: problems rarely belong to one domain alone.

What I hope to contribute

  • A disciplined, pressure-tested mindset shaped by aviation training.
  • Initiative and execution built through entrepreneurship and product development.
  • A collaborative style that values trust, ownership, and practical contribution.
  • A genuine interest in building solutions that are technically sound and humanly relevant.
