01 / Journey

One thread runs through
everything.

I keep moving toward situations where reliability is non-negotiable — and then building things that have to hold.

Secondary School

Started teaching because good tutoring was out of reach for most students.

A YouTube channel and community centre sessions — the first time I built something because I had seen the gap directly.

JC1 – JC2

Flight training turned discipline into instinct.

76+ hours at SYFC, 7 aviation theory papers, and a Final Flying Test cleared one week before JC2 prelims. The cockpit taught me that calm is something you build deliberately — one difficult situation at a time.

2023

EIC made engineering feel like something that owed the real world an answer.

Top 10 nationwide. Presented at the World Engineers Summit. The problem was elderly loneliness — and it was personal. My grandfather had suffered a spinal injury the year before.

2024

Leading EntreWeek showed me that ownership scales better than control.

My team wanted me to make every decision. I split them into groups that owned their parts instead. We engaged 85% of the school. REN Member of the Year — awarded to one person annually.

National Service

WebForce proved I build for users who cannot afford failure.

Magicians use it live, on stage, in front of audiences. It had to be reliable — not impressive in a demo, but trustworthy when it actually mattered. 60+ installs. 10 countries. SGD 1,700 revenue. Now scaling through a UK distributor.

Next

REP is the logical continuation.

The first environment that combines engineering depth, interdisciplinary thinking, and operating in the real world the way I already prefer to work.

04 / Fit

Why REP,
specifically.

01

Real problems ignore subject boundaries.

REP's interdisciplinary structure mirrors something I've learnt the hard way — in the cockpit, in product work, in leading a team through conflicting priorities. No hard problem I've encountered belonged entirely to one field.

02

The entrepreneurship track is not extra-curricular for me — it's operational.

WebForce is a functioning commercial product. ExpertConnect Asia placed me in environments with shifting briefs and no blueprint. I already work this way. REP builds the engineering foundation underneath it.

03

I want to build things people depend on — not just things that impress reviewers.

The standard in flying: the aircraft either holds or it doesn't. The standard in WebForce: the app either works on stage or it doesn't. REP is where I develop the engineering rigour to hold that standard at scale.

"Engineering earns its meaning when it serves people who actually need it."

✦
Calm under genuine pressure

Built over 76 hours in the air and a family crisis carried quietly through exam season. Not claimed — demonstrated.

⬡
Products that have to work

Every build I've shipped has had real stakes — performer trust, live audiences, paying users. I don't build for demos.

◈
Leadership through ownership

85% school engagement because the team felt it was theirs, not mine. Direction is easy — ownership is the work.
